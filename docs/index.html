How to read the waves + Focus Index

EEG frequency bands (Hz)

EEG, a measure of brain activity, is often summarized by how much power is present in specific frequency ranges:

  • Theta (4–8 Hz): increases with drowsiness and deep relaxation
  • Alpha (8–12 Hz): increases with relaxation and light meditation
  • Beta (13–30 Hz): increases with active concentration and attention control

What the Focus Index measures

NeuroTunes uses a simple, inspectable index based on frontal EEG:

FI = z(log(βα+θ))

Essentially, the ratio of beta to (alpha + theta), normalized for easy comparison between subjects.

Meaning,

  • Higher FI = relatively higher beta compared to alpha+theta.
  • Lower FI = relatively lower beta compared to alpha+theta.

What "stability" means

Stability is how much the Focus Index fluctuates during a clip.

  • More stable = FI changes less over time
  • Less stable = FI is more "jittery" over windows

This is a relative, bandpower-based metric computed from noisy EEG; it is not a clinical measure and can be affected by eye blinks, movement, or electrode contact.
